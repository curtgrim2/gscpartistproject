Celia Cruz is a singer born in Havana Cuba. The nickname "Queen of Salsa" was given to her due to her impact on the Salsa genre worldwide despite it being seen as a male dominated genre. She took a particular interest in music early on in her life, despite her father yearning for her to be a teacher. Her career flourished and climbed the ladder of prominence as she started off with singing on radio shows and elevated that to singing for Cuban Orchestra “La Sonora Matancera” and eventually her own music career. Her transferring over to the American market is paralleled with the fact that she was banned from setting foot in her home country due to her opposition to then Cuba dictator Fidel Castro. Despite this, she remained resilient and continued forward with her career. While in America, she began to promote and sing a mix of the Cuban and Afro Latin sound that became known as Salsa throughout the United States and the world. Cuba. Although she’s primarily known as a recording musical artist, Cruz also made a few film appearances in movies such as The Perez Family and The Mambo Kings. The honors that she has earned in her career includes 3 Grammy awards among multiple nominations as well as multiple tributes and honors from numerous industries and peers alike.
